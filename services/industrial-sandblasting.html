Industrial Sandblasting Services

Professional surface preparation for industrial equipment, structures, and components

What is Industrial Sandblasting?

Industrial sandblasting is a specialized surface preparation technique that uses compressed air to propel abrasive media at high velocity against a surface. This process effectively removes contaminants such as rust, old paint, scale, grease, and other unwanted materials from industrial equipment, structures, and components.

At Karph Blast and Paint, we utilize state-of-the-art sandblasting equipment and techniques to deliver superior results for your industrial applications. Our industrial sandblasting services are designed to prepare surfaces for painting, coating, or other finishing processes, ensuring maximum adhesion and longevity of the applied finishes.

Abrasive Media Options

Garnet

Natural mineral abrasive ideal for general cleaning and creating a medium surface profile. Less dust, recyclable, and environmentally friendly.

Steel Grit

Angular, durable abrasive for aggressive cleaning and profiling of steel surfaces. Recyclable and economical for large projects.

Aluminum Oxide

Extremely hard abrasive for aggressive cleaning and creating deep profiles. Ideal for preparing surfaces for thick coatings.

Glass Beads

Spherical media for cleaning without significant profile creation. Provides a satin finish and is ideal for delicate components.

Plastic Media

Gentle abrasive for removing coatings without damaging the substrate. Perfect for thin materials and sensitive components.

Soda

Water-soluble, biodegradable media for gentle cleaning. Ideal for historical restoration and environmentally sensitive areas.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our industrial sandblasting services

What types of industrial equipment can be sandblasted?

We can sandblast virtually any industrial equipment including storage tanks, pressure vessels, structural steel, heavy machinery, pipelines, heat exchangers, boilers, industrial vehicles, manufacturing equipment, and more. Our equipment and techniques can be adapted to accommodate various sizes and configurations.

How do you contain dust and debris during industrial sandblasting?

We implement comprehensive containment systems including physical barriers, negative air pressure environments, and industrial dust collection equipment. For enclosed spaces, we utilize vacuum blasting systems that capture the media and contaminants at the source. These measures ensure minimal environmental impact and prevent cross-contamination with nearby equipment or operations.

What surface cleanliness standards can you achieve?

We can achieve all industry-recognized cleanliness standards including SSPC (Society for Protective Coatings) and ISO standards. These range from SSPC-SP7/Brush-Off Blast for light cleaning to SSPC-SP5/White Metal Blast for the most thorough cleaning. The appropriate standard is determined based on your specific coating requirements and the operating environment of the equipment.

Can you sandblast equipment while our facility remains operational?

Yes, in many cases we can perform sandblasting while your facility remains operational. We coordinate closely with your operations team, implement proper containment measures, and schedule work during optimal periods to minimize disruption. For sensitive environments, we also offer off-site sandblasting services where equipment components can be transported to our facility.

How long does industrial sandblasting take?

Project duration varies significantly based on multiple factors including the size and complexity of the equipment, degree of contamination, accessibility, required cleanliness standard, and environmental conditions. Small components might be completed in hours, while large industrial structures could take days or weeks. We provide detailed timeline estimates following our initial assessment of your specific project.

Is industrial sandblasting environmentally safe?

Yes, our industrial sandblasting processes comply with all environmental regulations. We implement proper containment, use dust collection systems, and select appropriate abrasive media based on the project requirements. Additionally, we properly dispose of or recycle spent media and contaminants according to environmental guidelines. For particularly sensitive environments, we can utilize alternative eco-friendly media such as soda or crushed glass.
