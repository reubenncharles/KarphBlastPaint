Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our mobile services

What areas do your mobile services cover?

Our mobile services cover most of NSW. For larger projects or specialized services, we can extend our coverage area. Our various mobile crews throughout NSW allow us to efficiently service most locations in New South Wales, with travel charges typically applied based on distance for areas further from our headquarters. During your initial consultation, we'll discuss your specific location and any logistical considerations to provide accurate pricing and scheduling options.

How do you manage dust and debris during mobile operations?

We implement comprehensive containment measures tailored to each project's specific requirements and location. For standard abrasive blasting, we utilize portable containment structures with integrated vacuum recovery systems that capture up to 95% of spent media and debris at the source. For environmentally sensitive areas or locations near other operations, we can employ dustless blasting technology that virtually eliminates airborne dust by introducing water into the blasting stream. For large structural projects, we establish containment zones with shrouding, ground protection, and negative air pressure systems. All debris is properly collected and disposed of according to environmental regulations. Our technicians are trained in EPA-compliant dust control measures, and we modify our approach based on weather conditions, proximity to sensitive areas, and specific project requirements.

What utilities or site access do you require for mobile services?

Our mobile units are designed to operate with minimal site requirements, but certain considerations help optimize the process. Ideally, we need: adequate workspace around the item or structure being treated (generally 3-5 meters clearance); vehicle access to within 30-50 meters of the work area for equipment setup; and a water source for certain applications. While power access is beneficial, our units come equipped with generators when needed. For multi-day projects, secure overnight equipment storage is helpful. During our site assessment, we'll evaluate access points, identify any obstacles, and determine utility needs specific to your project. If your site has limitations, we'll develop alternative approachesâ€”we've successfully completed projects in challenging locations including rooftops, remote rural areas, and confined spaces by adapting our equipment and methodology.

How do weather conditions affect mobile sandblasting and painting?

Weather conditions significantly impact outdoor blasting and coating operations. Ideal conditions include temperatures between 10-35Â°C, humidity below 85%, and minimal wind. Rain obviously prevents outdoor work, and surfaces must be completely dry before coating application. High winds can compromise containment systems and affect blast pattern and coating application. Extreme temperatures affect coating cure times and performance. To manage these variables, we: closely monitor weather forecasts during project scheduling; establish temporary shelters for sensitive work when needed; use moisture meters to verify surface conditions; and adjust work hours to utilize optimal daily conditions. For critical timeline projects during uncertain weather periods, we develop contingency plans including portable shelters or sequenced approaches focusing on protected areas during adverse conditions. Our experience with New South Wales's climate allows us to effectively schedule projects during optimal seasonal windows.

Is mobile sandblasting as effective as workshop blasting?

Yes, our mobile sandblasting can achieve the same high-quality results as workshop operations. Our portable equipment delivers the same pressure, media flow, and technical capabilities as fixed installations. We achieve industry-standard cleanliness levels including SSPC-SP6 Commercial Blast, SSPC-SP10 Near-White Metal, and SSPC-SP5 White Metal as required for your specific coating system. The primary differences relate to environmental controls rather than quality outcomes. In mobile operations, we implement additional containment measures and may adjust work methods to accommodate weather and surrounding conditions. For extremely intricate items or projects requiring specialized processes like thermal coatings, workshop processing sometimes offers advantages. During consultation, we'll recommend the most appropriate approach based on your specific project requirements, but rest assured that our mobile services don't compromise on qualityâ€”just add convenience.

How do you handle permits and compliance for mobile work?

We manage all aspects of regulatory compliance for our mobile operations. This includes obtaining necessary work permits, environmental approvals, and safety certifications before beginning any project. Our team is well-versed in local council requirements, EPA regulations regarding dust control and waste disposal, and Safe Work Australia guidelines. For projects in specialized environments like heritage properties, waterways, or industrial facilities, we secure specific permits as required. We maintain comprehensive insurance coverage including public liability and professional indemnity insurance. During the planning phase, we identify all applicable compliance requirements for your specific location and project type, then handle the necessary documentation and approval processes. For projects on commercial or industrial sites, we coordinate with your safety personnel to ensure alignment with site-specific protocols and requirements.
