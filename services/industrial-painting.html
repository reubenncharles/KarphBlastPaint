What is Industrial Painting?

Industrial painting goes far beyond aesthetics—it's a critical process that protects valuable equipment and facilities from corrosion, chemical exposure, weather damage, and operational wear. Our industrial painting services utilize specialized high-performance coatings engineered to withstand harsh environments and extend the service life of your assets.

At Karph Blast and Paint, we bring decades of expertise to every industrial painting project. From preparation to application, we follow stringent industry standards to ensure exceptional adhesion, coverage, and durability. Our team is equipped to handle projects of all sizes, from individual equipment pieces to entire facilities, with minimal disruption to your operations.

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Answers to common questions about our industrial painting services

How long do industrial coatings typically last?

The lifespan of industrial coatings varies based on several factors including the coating system used, environmental conditions, substrate preparation quality, and operational stresses. In general, professionally applied industrial coatings can last 5-15 years before requiring maintenance. For example, epoxy systems in moderate environments may last 7-10 years, while polyurethane topcoats in exterior applications typically provide 5-7 years of service before maintenance is needed. Specialized systems like tank linings may require inspection every 3-5 years. We can provide specific expectations for your application during consultation.

Can you perform industrial painting while our facility remains operational?

Yes, in many cases we can schedule and execute industrial painting projects with minimal disruption to your operations. We achieve this through careful planning, containment systems to control overspray and fumes, and strategic scheduling during nights, weekends, or around shift changes. For more extensive projects, we can implement a phased approach, working on specific areas sequentially while allowing continued operation in others. Some specialized applications, particularly those involving internal tank linings or confined spaces, may require complete shutdown of specific equipment. We'll work closely with your operations team to develop a painting plan that minimizes impacts on productivity.

How do you ensure coating quality for industrial applications?

We maintain rigorous quality control processes throughout each industrial painting project. This includes thorough surface preparation verification (including blast profile measurement), environmental condition monitoring (temperature, humidity, dew point), wet film thickness testing during application, dry film thickness verification after curing, and adhesion testing. For specialized projects, we can also provide holiday testing (for pinhole detection), pull-off adhesion testing, and specific testing required by industry standards. Our technicians are trained in proper inspection techniques, and we document all quality control measures. Upon project completion, we provide comprehensive documentation of the quality control process for your records.

What preparation is required before industrial painting?

Proper surface preparation is the most critical factor for long-term coating performance. For industrial projects, we typically perform abrasive blasting to industry standards such as SSPC-SP6 (Commercial Blast), SSPC-SP10 (Near-White Metal), or SSPC-SP5 (White Metal) depending on the coating system and service environment. This removes all contaminants, old coatings, and creates the optimal surface profile for coating adhesion. Alternative preparation methods may include power tool cleaning, UHP water jetting, or chemical cleaning for specific situations. Prior to painting, we also ensure proper conditions for application including temperature, humidity control, and protection from contaminants. Our team will handle all aspects of preparation as part of our comprehensive service.

Do you provide warranties for industrial painting projects?

Yes, we provide warranties for our industrial painting projects. Standard workmanship warranties cover application quality and coating performance under normal operational conditions. Warranty periods typically range from 1-5 years depending on the coating system and environmental conditions. For larger projects or specialized applications, we can also facilitate manufacturer warranties that may extend coverage for specific coating systems. All warranties are clearly documented with specific terms and conditions. We recommend regular inspections and maintenance to maximize coating life, and we can establish ongoing maintenance programs to support the long-term performance of your industrial coatings beyond the warranty period.

How do you handle safety considerations during industrial painting?

Safety is our top priority on all industrial painting projects. Our comprehensive safety approach includes: detailed job safety analysis before project start, trained technicians with current safety certifications (including confined space entry and fall protection where applicable), proper respiratory protection and PPE, ventilation systems for fume control, explosion-proof equipment in volatile environments, containment systems to prevent environmental contamination, and compliance with all relevant regulations. We coordinate with facility safety personnel to ensure alignment with site-specific protocols, obtain necessary permits, and conduct regular safety meetings throughout the project. Our excellent safety record demonstrates our commitment to protecting both our team and your personnel during industrial painting operations.
